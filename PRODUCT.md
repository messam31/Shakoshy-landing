# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, one marketplace, served by a single marketing landing page:

- **Customers (primary, leads the page):** Homeowners and renters in Egypt who need a household service done — plumbing, electrical, AC, cleaning, carpentry, painting, aluminum & glass, moving, flooring, roof repair. They arrive with a problem and a budget worry ("how much will this cost, and can I trust whoever shows up?"). Mostly on mobile, often Arabic-first. The job to be done: find a trustworthy local pro fast, compare real offers, and hire with confidence.
- **Workers (secondary, but a first-class path):** Skilled local tradespeople who want a steady stream of nearby jobs, the ability to set their own price, and a reputation built on real reviews. The job to be done: understand that Shakoshy brings them paying work near them and lets them grow their business.

The page must speak to both without diluting either: a customer-led hero, a clear "Become a Worker" / "For Workers" path, and dual app downloads (Customer App + Worker App).

## Product Purpose

Shakoshy is a two-sided services marketplace app (Egypt). Customers describe a request and receive real offers from verified professionals; they compare price and reviews, then hire and track the job in-app. Workers receive nearby job opportunities, send offers at their own price, and build a reputation.

This website is the **marketing landing page** for that app. Its job is conversion, not utility: persuade customers to post a job / download the Customer App, and persuade workers to join / download the Worker App. Success = a visitor in either audience understands the value in seconds, trusts the platform, and takes the matching app/CTA action. Bilingual EN/AR (Arabic is the home market; full RTL).

## Brand Personality

Three words: **trustworthy, fast, transparent** — with a warm, local human touch (it's neighbors and tradespeople, not faceless gig labor).

- **Voice:** plain, confident, reassuring. Short verb-led promises ("Post your request for free", "Receive offers within minutes"). No jargon, no hype.
- **Emotional goal — customers:** relief and confidence. "My home is in good hands, I won't get ripped off, and someone reliable is on the way." Trust is the emotion that converts; price transparency and verification are the proof.
- **Emotional goal — workers:** opportunity and respect. "Real work near me, on my terms, where my reputation pays off."
- The orange brand (`#F07C0D`) carries energy and approachability; it should read friendly and dependable, never loud or cheap.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing template.** No gradient-blob hero, no hero-metric stat row, no endless identical icon-card grids, no tiny tracked uppercase eyebrow over every section. Shakoshy is a consumer services brand, not a B2B startup.
- **Cheap classifieds / low-trust listings board.** Dense, cluttered, ad-spammy marketplace aesthetics undercut the entire trust promise. Stay calm, spacious, curated.
- **Sterile corporate.** No cold, faceless enterprise tone — the brand is warm and local. Real people doing real work.
- **Overdesigned / flashy.** Heavy animation and loud gradients read as untrustworthy for a service you let into your home. Motion is purposeful and restrained.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the conversion lever.** Every section should reinforce safety: verified pros, transparent pricing, real reviews, secure in-app communication. Show proof, don't just claim it.
2. **Two audiences, one calm page.** Lead with the customer, but give workers a clear, unembarrassed path. Never make either side feel like an afterthought, and never crowd the page trying to serve both at once.
3. **Show the work, not abstractions.** Real services, real people, concrete outcomes (and concrete EGP costs). Favor honest imagery and plain promises over decorative metaphor.
4. **Bilingual and RTL are not an afterthought.** Arabic is the home market. Every layout, alignment, and motion choice must hold up identically in RTL; prefer logical properties throughout.
5. **Restraint earns trust.** Spacious, legible, quietly confident. Motion and color are deployed with intent; nothing flashy that would make a homeowner second-guess.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast (including placeholder text — watch muted gray on tinted/peach surfaces), large text ≥ 3:1, visible focus states on all interactive elements, full keyboard operability for nav, drawer, tabs, and search.
- **Full RTL parity (EN/AR)** with correct `dir`/`lang`, logical CSS properties, and mirrored directional assets.
- **Reduced motion is required.** Scroll-triggered reveals and any motion must have a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative (crossfade or instant), and content must never be gated on a reveal firing — sections render visible by default.
- Mobile-first: the primary customer audience is on phones; tap targets, legibility, and performance on mid-range devices matter.
